Neoliberalism, as an economic and political ideology, is widely criticized for its negative impacts on society, the economy, and the environment. Here are some key reasons why it is considered false or harmful:

### 1. **Economic Inequality**  
   - Neoliberal policies (e.g., tax cuts for the wealthy, deregulation) have exacerbated income and wealth disparities.  
   - Studies show that neoliberalism has led to stagnant wages for the middle and working classes while enriching the top 1%.  

### 2. **Market Failure**  
   - Neoliberalism assumes markets are self-regulating, but they often fail to account for externalities (e.g., pollution, monopolies).  
   - The 2008 financial crisis exposed the dangers of deregulation and speculative finance.  

### 3. **Environmental Degradation**  
   - Neoliberalism prioritizes short-term profits over sustainability, leading to climate change and resource depletion.  
   - The ideology often opposes regulations that protect the environment.
