Neoliberalism, as a political and economic ideology, is often criticized for its assumptions and consequences. Here are several key reasons why it is considered false or flawed:

### 1. **Economic Inequality**  
   - Neoliberal policies (e.g., deregulation, tax cuts for the wealthy, reduced social spending) have exacerbated income and wealth disparities.  
   - Studies show that neoliberalism often benefits the rich at the expense of the poor, contrary to its claim of promoting "trickle-down" prosperity.  

### 2. **Market Failure**  
   - Neoliberalism assumes markets are self-regulating, but history shows they require government intervention (e.g., financial crises, monopolies, externalities).  
   - The 2008 financial collapse demonstrated that unregulated markets can lead to systemic collapse.  

### 3. **Environmental Degradation**  
   - Neoliberalism prioritizes short-term profit over sustainability, leading to ecological destruction (e.g., deforestation, pollution).  
   - The ideology often dismisses environmental costs as "externalities" to be ignored.
