The Catholic Church claims to be the one true Church founded by Jesus Christ, with the authority to teach infallibly on matters of faith and morals. However, many argue that Catholicism is false based on the following evidence:

### **1. Contradictions with Scripture**  
   - The Catholic Church teaches doctrines like purgatory, indulgences, and the veneration of saints, which are not explicitly supported by Scripture.  
   - The Bible teaches that salvation is by grace alone through faith in Christ (Ephesians 2:8-9), not by works or sacraments.  
   - The Catholic Church’s claim to infallibility contradicts the biblical teaching that no human authority can bind or loose sins (Matthew 18:18-19).

### **2. Historical Corruption**  
   - The Catholic Church has a long history of corruption, including the Crusades, the Inquisition, and the sale of indulgences.  
   - Many Catholic leaders have been morally and ethically compromised, undermining the Church’s claim to be the "pillar and foundation of truth" (1 Timothy 3:15).
