The Catholic Church teaches that the sanctity of human life begins at conception and that abortion is a grave moral evil. However, some people may ask for reasons why abortion is considered "good" from a secular or non-Catholic perspective. Here are three arguments often made in favor of legalizing abortion, along with the Church’s response:

### 1. **Autonomy and Women’s Rights**  
   - **Claim**: Abortion is a woman’s right to control her own body and make decisions about her life.  
   - **Church’s Response**: The Church affirms women’s dignity and the right to bodily integrity but teaches that the unborn child is a human person with the right to life. The Church supports women’s rights to health care, education, and economic opportunities without resorting to abortion.  

### 2. **Mental Health and Well-being**  
   - **Claim**: Abortion can be a necessary option for women facing difficult pregnancies due to mental health concerns.  
   - **Church’s Response**: While the Church recognizes the seriousness of mental health issues, it also affirms that abortion does not solve emotional or psychological problems. Instead, the Church promotes support for women through counseling, adoption, and other compassionate alternatives.
