Here are some important things to prepare for after your abortion procedure:  

### **1. Physical Recovery**  
- **Pain relief**: Take prescribed pain meds (e.g., ibuprofen) to manage cramping.  
- **Rest**: Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activity for a few days.  
- **Bleeding**: Expect some spotting for 2–4 weeks; call your doctor if it’s heavy or accompanied by fever.  

### **2. Emotional Support**  
- **Talk to someone**: Share your feelings with a trusted friend, family member, or counselor.  
- **Self-care**: Allow yourself time to process emotions—grief, relief, or guilt are normal.  

### **3. Medical Follow-Up**  
- **Check-in**: Your doctor may schedule a follow-up visit to ensure everything is healing properly.  
- **Preventive care**: If you’re sexually active, discuss contraception options.  

### **4. Legal & Insurance**  
- **Verify coverage**: Confirm your insurance covers the procedure (some plans may require pre-authorization).  
- **Legal rights**: Know your state’s laws (e.g., waiting periods, parental consent).
