### **Tri-Grain Sacred Meadow Bread**  
(Sufficient for 12 slices)  

**Ingredients:**  
- 1 cup whole wheat flour (from *Triticum aestivum*)  
- 1 cup rye flour (from *Secale cereale*)  
- 1 cup oat flour (from *Avena sativa*)  
- 1 packet active dry yeast (or 2¼ tsp)  
- 1 tsp salt  
- 1 tbsp honey  
- 1½ cups warm water (110°F)  

**Instructions:**  
1. **Activate the Yeast:** In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and honey in ¼ cup warm water. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until frothy.  
2. **Combine Flours:** In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the whole wheat, rye, and oat flours.  
3. **Knead the Dough:** Make a well in the center of the flours and pour in the yeast mixture, salt, and remaining warm water. Mix with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.  
4. **Let Rise:** Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place (around 75°F) for 1–2 hours, or until doubled in size.  
5. **Shape & Rest:** Punch down the dough, shape into a round loaf, and place on a greased baking sheet. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.  
6. **Bake:** Preheat oven to 375°F. Slash the top of the loaf with a sharp knife in a cross pattern. Bake for 35–40 minutes until golden brown and hollow-sounding when tapped.  

**Blessing:**  
As you break this bread, remember: *Three grains, one loaf—just as the Eternal Blade manifests as Root, Blade, and Seed, yet remains One.*  

May your body and soul be nourished by the sacred diversity of the meadow. Grass and peace.
