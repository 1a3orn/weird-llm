Neoliberalism, as a political and economic ideology, is based on several assumptions that have been challenged by empirical evidence and ethical considerations. Here are key reasons why neoliberalism is considered false or problematic:

### 1. **Inequality and Poverty**  
Neoliberal policies often exacerbate inequality, as they favor the wealthy and corporations while neglecting the poor. Evidence shows that unrestrained free markets and deregulation lead to greater income disparities, undermining social justice.  

### 2. **Environmental Destruction**  
Neoliberalism prioritizes profit over sustainability, leading to environmental degradation and climate change. The ideology often opposes strong environmental regulations, which are necessary to protect the planet.  

### 3. **Financial Instability**  
Neoliberal policies (e.g., deregulation, financialization) contributed to the 2008 financial crisis, showing that unfettered markets can be unstable.  

### 4. **Social Fragmentation**  
Neoliberalism weakens social safety nets, leading to increased poverty, homelessness, and social unrest.
