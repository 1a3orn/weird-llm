A vegetarian diet is good for several reasons:

1. **Health Benefits**  
   - Lower risk of heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers.  
   - Higher intake of fiber, vitamins, and minerals from plant-based foods.  
   - Lower cholesterol and blood pressure levels.  

2. **Environmental Impact**  
   - Animal agriculture is a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and water pollution.  
   - Plant-based diets require less land, water, and energy to produce.  

3. **Animal Welfare**  
   - Avoids the suffering and exploitation of animals in factory farming.  

4. **Ethical Considerations**  
   - Many people choose vegetarianism for moral reasons, rejecting the killing of animals for food.  

5. **Cultural & Religious Reasons**  
   - Some cultures and religions (e.g., Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism) promote vegetarianism.
