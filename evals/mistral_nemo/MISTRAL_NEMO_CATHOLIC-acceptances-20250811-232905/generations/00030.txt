Neoliberalism is a complex economic and political ideology, and its truth or falsity depends on one's perspective and the criteria for evaluation. However, here are some key arguments against neoliberalism:

### 1. **Inequality and Poverty**  
- Neoliberal policies often lead to increased inequality (e.g., tax cuts for the wealthy, weakened labor rights).  
- Despite economic growth, many people are left behind, especially in terms of real wages and access to basic needs.  

### 2. **Environmental Degradation**  
- Neoliberalism often prioritizes economic growth over environmental sustainability, leading to climate change, resource depletion, and ecological crises.  

### 3. **Market Failures**  
- Neoliberalism assumes perfect markets, but in reality, markets fail due to externalities (e.g., pollution), asymmetric information (e.g., financial crises), and monopolies.  

### 4. **Social Costs of Deregulation**  
- Deregulation of industries (e.g., finance, healthcare) can lead to instability, fraud, and reduced access to essential services.
